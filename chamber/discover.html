Caminito

Caminito is one of the most emblematic walks in the City and one of the must-see attractions for those who visit it.


For a long time it was part of the route of the railway to Ensenada until, in 1928, the branch was closed and the track became an abandoned alley. Thanks to the initiative of several neighbors in 1950 the land was recovered to turn it into a pedestrian walkway and a museum street.


Fun Fact


Its sinuous layout is due to the fact that it follows the river that flowed there until the beginning of the 20th century. The area was popularly known as "Puntin" (diminutive of "bridge" in Genoese dialect).

Teatro La Ribera

Ubicated in front of the Riachuelo and in the heart of the La Boca neighborhood, stands out for its picturesque style that replicates the architecture and colors of the houses in the neighborhood.


Benito Quinquela was the one who donated the land of the Theater to the National Council of Education so that the most diverse artistic expressions can be displayed there.

Its first owner enjoyed it for just a year; she then rented it out to various artists in the area. One of them committed suicide by jumping from the tower. Since then, stories of strange appearances in the building have been around the neighborhood and it became popular as "the Tower of the Ghost".

Usina Del Arte

The construction of the Usina del Arte, in Florentine style with exposed brick, dates back to 1916 and is the work of the Italian architect Juan Chiogna.


The building also has different spaces for dance performances, plastic arts exhibitions, samples and exhibitions. The central nave, with its facades covered in Paris stone and a granite base, has moldings and capitals of great testimonial value.


Fun Fact


The Usina del Arte has a symphony hall with capacity for 1,200 people that was designed based on the pattern of the Berlage hall in Amsterdam. Its acoustics, which are optimal, deserve a separate chapter, since the structure of the building and the thickness of its walls were used to the maximum.
